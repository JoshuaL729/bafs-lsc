Core Concepts

The essential building blocks of Chapter 20

Section 20.1

Introduction

Not all accounting errors leave the trial balance unaffected. Chapter 20 focuses on errors that cause the trial balance to disagree — where debit and credit totals are unequal.

Key Rule: When a double entry is made with different amounts in the ledger accounts, the trial balance will disagree.
Section 20.2

Suspense Account

A temporary account opened to record the difference between the debit and credit totals of a disagreed trial balance, enabling financial statements to be prepared.

If debit total < credit total:
  Dr Suspense (shortage on debit side)

If credit total < debit total:
  Cr Suspense (shortage on credit side)
Important: The suspense account is closed to zero once all errors are found and corrected. It is shown separately in the Statement of Financial Position.
Section 20.3

Errors Affecting Trial Balance

Five types of errors cause the trial balance to disagree. Each requires a correcting entry involving the suspense account to close it off.

The 5 Types:
1. Casting error in a book of original entry
2. Single entry (one side missing)
3. Double entry of unequal amounts
4. Incorrect casting of an account
5. Incorrect listing of balance in trial balance
Section 20.4

Adjustments of Profits

Some errors affect the net profit (and sometimes gross profit) while others do not. Only errors affecting income statement items impact profit.

Net profit as per draft accounts
Add: Errors that understated profit
Less: Errors that overstated profit
─────────────────────
= Corrected net profit
Section 20.5

Adjustments of Working Capital

Working capital = Current assets − Current liabilities. Only errors affecting current assets or current liabilities impact working capital.

Working capital as per draft accounts
Add: Errors that understated WC
Less: Errors that overstated WC
─────────────────────
= Corrected working capital
Section 20.6

Other Transactions

Special transactions commonly tested in HKDSE exams alongside error corrections.

Contra Entry: When a trade debtor is also a trade creditor — set off balances:
Dr Trade payables / Cr Trade receivables

Donation of Goods: Record at cost, not sales value:
Dr Donation expense / Cr Purchases
⚠️

The 5 Error Types Explained

Click each error to see analysis and correcting entries

① Casting Error in a Book of Original Entry

The total of a day book (e.g., sales journal, purchases journal) is calculated incorrectly. The debit and credit entries are therefore of unequal amounts.

Example: Sales journal cast as $50,400 instead of $54,400.

Entries made: Dr Trade receivables $54,400 / Cr Sales $50,400 → Shortage of $4,000 on credit side.

Before error found: Cr Suspense $4,000
Correcting entry: Dr Suspense $4,000 / Cr Sales $4,000
② Single Entry (One Side Missing)

Only one side of the double entry is recorded — either the debit or the credit is missing entirely.

Example: Cash payment of $1,680 to K Lee — only Cr Cash $1,680 recorded, no debit entry.

Shortage of $1,680 on debit side → Dr Suspense $1,680.

Correcting entry: Dr K Lee $1,680 / Cr Suspense $1,680
③ Double Entry of Unequal Amounts

Both debit and credit entries are made, but for different amounts.

Example: Goods purchased from K Yu for $12,000 — Dr Purchases $12,000 / Cr K Yu $1,200. Shortage of $10,800 on credit side.

Before error found: Cr Suspense $10,800
Correcting entry: Dr Suspense $10,800 / Cr K Yu $10,800
④ Incorrect Casting of an Account

The balance of a ledger account is calculated incorrectly, resulting in an incorrect balance being carried forward to the trial balance.

Example: Drawings account debit side cast as $10,000 instead of $11,000 → debit balance understated by $1,000.

Before error found: Dr Suspense $1,000
Correcting entry: Dr Drawings $1,000 / Cr Suspense $1,000
⑤ Incorrect Listing of Balance in Trial Balance

A balance is placed on the wrong side of the trial balance (e.g., a debit balance listed as a credit balance).

Example: Carriage outwards debit balance of $2,400 listed on the credit side → shortage of $4,800 on debit side.

Before error found: Dr Suspense $4,800
Correcting entry: No debit entry / Cr Suspense $4,800
Then: Place balance on correct (debit) side of trial balance.

Note: No correcting entry is made in the trial balance itself — it is not a ledger account.

When a trade debtor is also a trade creditor, their balances can be set off via a contra entry.

Details Dr ($) Cr ($)
Trade payables — [Name] (Trade payables ledger) X
Trade receivables — [Name] (Trade receivables ledger) X
Contra entry to set off balances where the same party is both debtor and creditor.
Key Point: A contra entry does NOT affect trial balance agreement (both debit and credit entries are of equal amounts). It does NOT affect net profit (no nominal accounts involved). It DOES affect working capital if the amounts differ — reducing both current assets and current liabilities.

When a firm donates goods to a charity — record at cost, not sales value.

Details Dr ($) Cr ($)
Donation expense Cost of goods
Purchases Cost of goods
Goods donated to charity at cost price (not sales value).
Common Exam Error: Students often record the donation at sales value. Always use the cost of the goods donated. If the error was recorded at sales value, you must reverse the incorrect amount and record the correct cost.

Glossary

All 8 key terms from Chapter 20

Suspense Account
暫記帳戶
A temporary account used to record the difference between the debit and credit totals of a disagreed trial balance. It is closed to zero once all errors are found and corrected.
Example: If the trial balance has a $500 shortage on the debit side, Dr Suspense $500.
Trial Balance
試算表
A statement listing all ledger account balances to verify that total debits equal total credits. It is prepared before financial statements.
A trial balance that does not agree indicates one or more errors affecting the equality of debits and credits.
Casting Error
加總錯誤
An arithmetic error in totalling (casting) a column in a book of original entry or a ledger account, resulting in an incorrect total being posted.
Sales journal cast as $50,400 instead of $54,400 — a casting error of $4,000.
Single Entry
單邊入帳
An error where only one side of the double entry is recorded — either the debit or the credit entry is missing entirely from the ledger.
Cash paid to a creditor — only Cr Cash recorded, no Dr Creditor entry made.
Working Capital
流動資金 / 營運資金
The difference between current assets and current liabilities. A positive figure represents net current assets; a negative figure represents net current liabilities.
Current assets $50,000 − Current liabilities $30,000 = Working capital $20,000.
Contra Entry
對銷分錄
An entry used to set off the balance of a trade debtor against the balance of the same party as a trade creditor. Dr Trade payables / Cr Trade receivables.
Jason Ltd owes the firm $12,000 (debtor) and the firm owes Jason Ltd $4,200 (creditor) — a contra entry can set off $4,200.
Net Profit Adjustment
淨利潤調整
A statement showing corrections to the draft net profit to arrive at the corrected net profit, by adding back understated items and deducting overstated items.
Draft net profit $25,800 + Purchases overcast $200 − Discounts received overcast $930 = Corrected net profit $25,070.
Donation of Goods
捐贈貨品
When a firm donates goods to a charity, the entry is recorded at the cost of the goods (not sales value): Dr Donation expense / Cr Purchases.
Goods costing $3,000 (sold for $5,000) donated: Dr Donation expense $3,000 / Cr Purchases $3,000.

HKDSE Exam Tips

Proven strategies for Chapter 20 questions

1

Always Check Whether the Error Affects the Suspense Account First

Ask: "Are the debit and credit entries of the same amount?" If yes → does NOT affect the suspense account. If no → DOES affect the suspense account. This is the most common source of marks lost in Paper 2A.

2

Use the 3-Step Approach for Net Profit Corrections

Step 1: Write all correcting journal entries. Step 2: Highlight entries in nominal accounts (revenue/expense). Step 3: If a nominal account is credited → increase net profit; if debited → decrease net profit. This saves time and prevents errors.

3

After Closing Entries — Use Profit & Loss Account, Not Nominal Accounts

If errors are found after closing entries have been made, correcting entries must be made in the Profit and Loss account (e.g., "P&L — Sales", "P&L — Purchases") instead of the original nominal accounts, which have already been closed off.

4

Do NOT Make Correcting Entries in the Trial Balance

The trial balance is NOT a ledger account. For an incorrect listing error (Error Type 5), you only need to: (a) make the suspense account entry, and (b) place the balance on the correct side of the trial balance. No journal entry touches the trial balance itself.

5

Working Capital: Only Current Assets and Current Liabilities Matter

Errors affecting non-current assets, non-current liabilities, or income statement items only do NOT affect working capital. Only ask: "Does this error affect a current asset or current liability?" If yes → working capital is affected.

6

Donation of Goods — Always Use Cost, Never Sales Value

A very common trap in HKDSE exams. The question will give you both the cost and the sales value of donated goods. Always record at cost: Dr Donation expense (cost) / Cr Purchases (cost). If the error was recorded at sales value, reverse the incorrect entry first, then record the correct one.

7

Narrations Are Usually NOT Required in HKDSE Exams

The marking scheme for HKDSE Paper 2A journal entries typically does not award marks for narrations. Save time by skipping them unless the question explicitly asks for narrations.
