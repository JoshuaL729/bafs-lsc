Core Concepts

The essential building blocks of Chapter 20

Section 20.1

Introduction

Not all accounting errors leave the trial balance unaffected. Chapter 20 focuses on errors that cause the trial balance to disagree.

Key Rule: When a double entry is made with different amounts, the TB will disagree.
Section 20.2

Suspense Account

A temporary account opened to record the difference between debit and credit totals of a disagreed trial balance.

Dr shortage? Dr Suspense
Cr shortage? Cr Suspense
Section 20.3

The 5 Error Types

1. Casting error in day book
2. Single entry missing
3. Double entry unequal amounts
4. Incorrect account casting
5. Incorrect listing in TB

Section 20.4

Profit Adjustments

Only errors affecting nominal accounts (income/expense) impact profit. Use P&L account for corrections after closing.

Section 20.5

Working Capital

Working capital = Current assets − Current liabilities. Only errors in these categories impact the final WC figure.

Section 20.6

Other Transactions

Contra Entry: Dr Payables / Cr Receivables.
Donation of Goods: Record at cost: Dr Donation / Cr Purchases.

⚠️

The 5 Error Types Explained

Analysis and correcting entries

① Casting Error in a Book of Original Entry

Total of a day book calculated incorrectly. Debit and credit entries are unequal.

Example: Sales journal overcast by $4,000.
Correcting entry: Dr Suspense $4,000 / Cr Sales $4,000
② Single Entry (One Side Missing)

Only one side of the double entry is recorded.

Example: Cash paid $1,680 to K Lee — no debit entry made.
Correcting entry: Dr K Lee $1,680 / Cr Suspense $1,680
③ Double Entry of Unequal Amounts

Both debit and credit entries are made, but for different amounts.

Example: Purchase $12,000 recorded as Cr Creditor $1,200.
Correcting entry: Dr Suspense $10,800 / Cr Creditor $10,800
④ Incorrect Casting of an Account

The balance of a ledger account is calculated incorrectly.

Example: Drawings account cast as $10,000 instead of $11,000.
Correcting entry: Dr Drawings $1,000 / Cr Suspense $1,000
⑤ Incorrect Listing of Balance in Trial Balance

A balance is placed on the wrong side of the trial balance.

Note: No correcting journal entry is made in the TB itself. Correct the suspense account then move the balance to the correct side.
